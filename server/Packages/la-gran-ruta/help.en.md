# The Great Route

A road-trip card game: be the first to complete **exactly 1,000 kilometres**. Several hands are played; the first player past **5,000 points** wins the match.

## Your turn

1. **Draw** a card (Space).
2. **Play** one card (Enter) **or discard** one (Delete).

Your hand is your command post: arrows walk the cards, Shift+F10 sorts or filters to the playable ones, and Escape brings you back to it from anywhere. **S** tells you how you are doing (kilometres, state, immunities); **C** does the same. **Shift+S** runs through the rivals in one go (your own you already get with S), and the players panel speaks each one's state.

## The cards

- **Kilometres** (25, 50, 75, 100, 200): advance your car. You can only play them while *rolling*, and never past the goal (the finish is exact). Only two 200s per hand.
- **Attacks**: thrown at a *rolling* rival — Red light, Out of fuel, Flat tyre and Accident stop them; the Speed limit keeps them under 50 until they lift it.
- **Remedies**: cure your own problem — Green light (starts you or clears the red light), Fuel, Spare wheel, Repairs and End of limit. Mind: repairing does **not** restart you — after Fuel, the Spare wheel or Repairs you stay stopped until you play a Green light (unless you hold Right of way). End of limit does free you directly.
- **Immunities** (one of each): shield you forever from one problem and clear it if present. *Right of way* shields you from the red light **and** the speed limit.

## The coup fourré

If you are attacked while HOLDING the matching immunity, you may answer on the spot: the attack is cancelled, you bank a big bonus **and the turn becomes yours**.

## Scoring a hand

1 point per kilometre; 100 per immunity played (300 extra for all four); 300 per coup fourré; 400 for completing the trip; 300 for a safe trip (no 200s); 300 for completing on an exhausted deck; 500 for shutting a rival out at zero.

You start each hand **stopped**: you need a Green light (or Right of way) to get rolling.

## Team play

In a team game your team shares **one car**: kilometres, hazards, immunities and points are common. Each member draws and plays **their own cards** (your hand is secret even from your partner) and turns always alternate between teams. If your team is attacked and your PARTNER holds the shield, the coup fourré is theirs.
