# Snakes and Ladders

A tabletop classic for 2 to 8 players: a single 100-square track folded into a
serpentine. There are no decisions to make — you roll the die, your piece
advances, and the board does the rest. First to reach square 100 wins.

## How to play

- On your turn, roll the die (Enter or the **Roll dice** button). Your piece
  advances that many squares.
- Pieces start **off the board** (the start tray): the first roll enters the
  track at square 1 onwards.
- Land on the **foot of a ladder** and you climb to its top square.
- Land on a **snake's mouth** and you slide down to its tail.
- Finishing requires reaching square 100 **by exact count**. If the roll
  overshoots, the piece reaches 100 and **bounces back** the excess (from 98,
  a 5 leaves you on 97).
- Roll a **6 and you roll again**.
- When someone reaches the goal the game continues to decide second and third
  place; it ends when a single player remains on the track.

## Keyboard

- **Left/right arrows**: walk the track square by square (the numbering always
  advances, even where the visual row reverses direction).
- **Up/down arrows**: jump a whole row (±10 squares).
- **Digits**: typing a number jumps straight to that square (for example, 4
  then 7 goes to square 47).
- **M**: go to your piece. **N / Shift+N**: cycle the squares holding pieces.
- The start tray (off the board) sits left of square 1.
- Every square announces its number, whether it is a snake's mouth or a
  ladder's foot (and where it leads), and which pieces stand on it.
