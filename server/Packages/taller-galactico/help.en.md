# Galactic Workshop

You are the chief engineer of a space workshop. The first player to get **all four of
their ship's systems operational** — the **reactor**, the **life support**, the **data
core** and the **hull frame** — wins. Your rivals will try to break them; you can repair,
shield and strike back.

## Help during play

- **F1** opens this Galactic Workshop guide.
- **Ctrl+F1** opens the complete list of shortcuts available in this game.
- **Ctrl+Shift+F1** shows the active rules, such as hand size and systems required to win.
- With a card in your hand focused, **Shift+F1** opens its help and explains what it does and which targets it accepts.

## Your turn

1. Play **one** card from your hand, **or** discard 1 to 3 face-down.
2. You automatically draw back up to 3 cards and the turn passes.

If you have no cards and nothing to do, your turn is a "pass" and you still refill.

## The cards

- **Modules** (reactor, life support, data core, hull frame): installed in your workshop.
  Only one of each system. The **universal module** is a joker: it counts as whichever
  system you are missing.
- **Breakdowns** (overload, oxygen leak, data corruption, structural crack): thrown at a
  rival's module of the same system. A broken module doesn't count toward winning; a
  **second breakdown destroys it** for scrap. The **systems failure** hits any module.
- **Repairs** (coolant, sealant, debugger, bracing): on your own module. If it's broken,
  they fix it. If it's healthy, they **shield** it (the next breakdown burns the shield
  instead of the module). A **second repair on a shielded module certifies it**: untouchable
  forever. **Nanobots** fit any system.
- **Specials**:
  - **Exchange crane**: swap one of your modules for a rival's (breakdowns and shields
    travel along; certified modules can't move).
  - **Imperial requisition**: take a rival's non-certified module into your free system.
  - **Chain leak**: your breakdowns jump onto rivals' clean modules, system by system.
  - **Imperial inspection**: everyone else discards their whole hand.
  - **Total trade-in**: swap your entire workshop with a rival's, certified modules included.

## Fine print

- The discard pile is **face-down**: nobody knows what you threw, and when the draw pile
  runs dry it is reshuffled and served again.
- A broken module isn't lost: repair it and it counts again.
- The first workshop with its **four distinct systems operational** wins (shielded and
  certified count; broken don't).

**S** tells you how your workshop is doing; **Shift+S** runs through the rival workshops
in one go (your own you already get with S). Press **Ctrl+F1** for the full list of
keyboard shortcuts.

## Playing with a screen reader

### Your hand

- **Up Arrow** and **Down Arrow** move through the cards; **Home** and **End** go to the ends. **Right Arrow** enters the actions, **Shift+F10** opens the menu and **Escape** returns to the hand.
- **Shift+F1** opens the focused card's help. **Enter** plays it and **Delete** offers to discard it; the hand refills automatically when the turn ends.
- If a card needs a system or rival, a dialog opens with its legal targets and the reason for the choice.

### Checking the workshop

- **S** announces your four systems and their state; **Shift+S** reviews the rival workshops. **Ctrl+P** moves to the players list.
- Installations, breakdowns, repairs, shields, certifications, discards and private draws are announced automatically.

### Moving between areas and using chat

- **F6** cycles through the hand, players and connection; non-modal dialogs and chat join while open. **Shift+F6** moves in reverse.
- **Ctrl+Shift+R** opens chat and focuses the message box. **Ctrl+Shift+H** opens or closes chat.
- Help and rules are reading documents. Use your screen reader's heading and list commands, then close them with **Escape** to return to play.
